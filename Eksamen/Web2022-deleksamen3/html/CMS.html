CMS

CMS is a term that most are familiar with today. It stands for content management system and is widely known for its ability to help users manage their digital content without specialized technical knowledge. There are two types of CMS - traditional and headless. Traditional CMS has a monolithic structure, where the backend (database) and the frontend (user interface) are tied together. While headless CMS is not connected to the frontend directly, it uses API (application programing interface). Both of the types are valuable depending on your goal, but how do you know which software is optimal for you?

There is a whole variety of CMS applications to choose from, but you need to know the differences between the two main types before that. In this article we will be looking at the differences of the traditional CMS and the headless CMS types. We will explore a couple examples of the CMS solutions that are out there on the web.

Traditional CMS has been around since the start of web development. While it is still being used to this current day, it is more appealing to small business owners, than bigger companies. You may have already heard of a couple well known CMS solutions - WordPress, Dupal, Sitecore. These platforms are known for its simple usability, and that it is accessible for everyone.

Since traditional CMS is a monolith, it allows all of the website's contents to be stored in a database, while at the same time you can choose the design you want to display the contents in. This type of CMS is based a lot around templates and plug-ins, to help the user get the right web page outline, while customizing around its design.The biggest downside of traditional CMS, is that it has limited flexibility and is mostly focused around websites.

Unlike the traditional CMS, headless CMS has no direct contact with frontend. Traditional CMS stores all the necessary contents and allows API to display them on whatever screen or electronic device the company wants to run it on.

Although headless CMS uses up more time and money, it opens a bigger window for growth and makes adjusting to developing technology easier. With softwares like Sanity and Contentful companies can achieve customer-first experiences, with a bit of help from a person that can work their way in developing.

And if you want to have more customizable applications that reach out to more people, then a headless software is a fit for you.
